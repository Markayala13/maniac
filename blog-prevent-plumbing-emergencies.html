How to Prevent Plumbing Emergencies in San Fernando Valley

Most plumbing emergencies can be prevented with proper maintenance and awareness. Here's your guide to keeping your plumbing in top shape.

Watch What Goes Down Your Drains

Never put grease, oil, coffee grounds, or food scraps down kitchen drains. In bathrooms, only flush toilet paper - no wipes, even if labeled "flushable." Use drain strainers and hair catchers to prevent clogs.

Regular Maintenance Checks

Monthly: Check under sinks for leaks, test water pressure, clean drain stoppers. Quarterly: Flush your water heater, inspect visible pipes. Annually: Get a professional inspection of your entire plumbing system.

Protect Pipes from Temperature Changes

During cold snaps, let faucets drip and keep cabinet doors open. Disconnect outdoor hoses and insulate exposed pipes. During heat waves, monitor for sudden increases in your water bill indicating leaks.

Know the Warning Signs

Call a professional immediately if you notice water stains, sewer odors, multiple slow drains, sudden pressure changes, or unusual sounds from pipes.
